Malaria Information

About Malaria

Malaria is a mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by parasites of the genus Plasmodium. It is transmitted through the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes.

Causes of Malaria

Malaria is caused by the bite of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. These mosquitoes carry the Plasmodium parasite in their saliva, which is transmitted to humans during a mosquito bite.

Symptoms of Malaria

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Sweating
  • Headaches
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Muscle pain and fatigue
  • Joint pain

Home Remedies for Malaria

1. Stay hydrated with plenty of fluids

2. Rest and get plenty of sleep

3. Use mosquito nets while sleeping

4. Take anti-malarial herbal supplements like Artemisia annua (Sweet wormwood)

5. Drink ginger or cinnamon tea to reduce fever

Medicines for Malaria

Common medicines for treating malaria include:

  • Chloroquine
  • Artemisinin-based combination therapies (ACTs)
  • Quinine
  • Mefloquine
  • Atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone)

Foods to Include in Diet

A healthy diet can help strengthen the immune system and aid in recovery from malaria. Include the following foods:

  • Fruits rich in Vitamin C (e.g., oranges, kiwi)
  • Leafy greens (e.g., spinach, kale)
  • Lean proteins (e.g., chicken, fish)
  • Whole grains (e.g., brown rice, quinoa)
  • Nuts and seeds (e.g., almonds, chia seeds)
  • Plenty of water and herbal teas
