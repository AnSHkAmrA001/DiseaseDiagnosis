Sinusitis Information

About Sinusitis

Sinusitis, also known as a sinus infection or rhinosinusitis, is an inflammation or swelling of the tissue lining the sinuses. It can be acute, lasting up to four weeks, or chronic, lasting longer than twelve weeks, despite treatment efforts. Acute sinusitis is often caused by a viral infection, while chronic sinusitis may have multiple underlying factors.

Causes of Sinusitis

Sinusitis can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Viral infections (common cold, flu)
  • Bacterial infections (less common)
  • Allergic reactions (allergic rhinitis)
  • Nasal polyps (small growths in the nasal passages)
  • Deviated nasal septum (crooked or shifted nasal cartilage)
  • Environmental irritants (smoke, air pollution)
  • Immune system deficiencies

Symptoms of Sinusitis

  • Nasal congestion
  • Facial pain or pressure
  • Headache
  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Loss of smell
  • Cough
  • Sore throat
  • Fatigue
  • Fever
  • Bad breath

Home Remedies for Sinusitis

1. Use saline nasal irrigation or saline nasal sprays to help clear nasal passages.

2. Apply warm compresses to the face to relieve sinus pressure.

3. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids.

4. Use over-the-counter nasal decongestants or antihistamines as directed.

5. Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air.

6. Get plenty of rest to help your body fight the infection.

Medicines for Sinusitis

Medications for sinusitis may include:

  • Antibiotics for bacterial sinus infections (if prescribed by a doctor)
  • Nasal corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and swelling
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers for pain and fever
  • Antihistamines for allergic sinusitis
  • Decongestants to relieve nasal congestion

Diet Recommendations for Sinusitis

There are no specific dietary recommendations for sinusitis. However, staying hydrated and eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains can help support overall health and immune function.
