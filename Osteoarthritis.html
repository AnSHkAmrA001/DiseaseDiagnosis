Osteoarthritis Information

About Osteoarthritis

Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease characterized by the breakdown of cartilage in one or more joints. It is the most common form of arthritis and typically affects weight-bearing joints such as the knees, hips, and spine.

Symptoms of Osteoarthritis

The symptoms of osteoarthritis can vary depending on the affected joint(s) and the severity of the condition. Common symptoms include:

  • Joint pain
  • Swelling and tenderness
  • Stiffness, especially after periods of inactivity
  • Decreased range of motion
  • Grating sensation or popping sounds (crepitus) during joint movement

Causes of Osteoarthritis

The exact cause of osteoarthritis is unknown, but several factors may contribute to its development, including:

  • Age-related wear and tear on the joints
  • Joint injuries or trauma
  • Obesity
  • Genetics
  • Joint overuse or repetitive movements

Treatment for Osteoarthritis

Treatment for osteoarthritis focuses on relieving symptoms and improving joint function. Common treatment options include:

  • Medications to reduce pain and inflammation
  • Physical therapy to strengthen muscles and improve joint mobility
  • Weight management to reduce stress on the joints
  • Assistive devices such as braces or splints
  • In severe cases, surgery may be recommended, such as joint replacement or joint fusion

Prevention of Osteoarthritis

While osteoarthritis cannot always be prevented, there are steps you can take to reduce your risk or delay the onset of symptoms:

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Stay physically active with low-impact exercises
  • Avoid overuse or repetitive movements that stress the joints
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in nutrients that support joint health
  • Protect your joints during physical activities
