Strep Throat Information

About Strep Throat

Strep throat is a bacterial infection that causes inflammation and pain in the throat. It is most common in children and adolescents, but it can affect people of all ages. Strep throat is caused by group A Streptococcus bacteria, and it can spread through respiratory droplets from coughing, sneezing, or sharing utensils.

Causes of Strep Throat

Strep throat is caused by infection with group A Streptococcus bacteria. Risk factors for strep throat include:

  • Close contact with an infected person
  • Living in crowded conditions
  • Compromised immune system
  • Poor hygiene practices

Symptoms of Strep Throat

  • Sore throat
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Swollen and tender lymph nodes in the neck
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Rash (scarlet fever)
  • Nausea or vomiting (more common in children)
  • White patches or streaks on the tonsils

Home Remedies for Strep Throat

1. Get plenty of rest to help your body recover.

2. Drink warm liquids like tea with honey or broth to soothe the throat.

3. Gargle with warm salt water to reduce throat inflammation.

4. Use over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen or ibuprofen for pain and fever.

5. Use a cool-mist humidifier to add moisture to the air and ease throat discomfort.

Medicines for Strep Throat

Antibiotics are typically prescribed to treat strep throat and prevent complications. Common antibiotics for strep throat include penicillin, amoxicillin, and azithromycin. It's important to complete the full course of antibiotics as prescribed by your healthcare provider, even if symptoms improve.

Diet Recommendations for Strep Throat

When you have strep throat, it's important to stay hydrated and eat foods that are easy to swallow and gentle on the throat:

  • Warm soups and broths
  • Soft fruits like applesauce and bananas
  • Cool and smooth foods like yogurt and pudding
  • Avoid spicy, acidic, or rough-textured foods that can irritate the throat.
