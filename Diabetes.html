Diabetes Mellitus Information

About Diabetes Mellitus

Diabetes mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels (hyperglycemia) resulting from defects in insulin production, insulin action, or both. There are several types of diabetes, including type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.

Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1 Diabetes: An autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks and destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas, leading to insulin deficiency.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: A metabolic disorder characterized by insulin resistance, where cells do not respond effectively to insulin, and progressive insulin deficiency over time.
  • Gestational Diabetes: A type of diabetes that occurs during pregnancy, typically in the second or third trimester, and increases the risk of complications for both the mother and baby.

Symptoms of Diabetes

The symptoms of diabetes can vary depending on the type and severity of the condition, but common symptoms include:

  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Extreme hunger
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow wound healing
  • Tingling or numbness in the hands or feet

Complications of Diabetes

Untreated or poorly managed diabetes can lead to a range of complications affecting various organs and systems in the body, including:

  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Neuropathy (nerve damage)
  • Nephropathy (kidney disease)
  • Retinopathy (eye disease)
  • Peripheral vascular disease
  • Foot ulcers and amputations
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS)

Management of Diabetes

The management of diabetes involves lifestyle modifications, medication, and regular monitoring to achieve and maintain target blood sugar levels. Common treatment options include:

  • Insulin therapy for type 1 diabetes and sometimes for type 2 diabetes
  • Oral medications to lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity
  • Dietary changes, such as limiting carbohydrates and sugar intake, and focusing on balanced meals
  • Regular physical activity to improve blood sugar control, weight management, and overall health
  • Monitoring blood sugar levels regularly using glucometers and other devices
  • Education and support to help individuals manage their diabetes effectively and prevent complications

Prevention of Diabetes

While type 1 diabetes cannot be prevented, type 2 diabetes and gestational diabetes can often be delayed or prevented through lifestyle modifications and healthy habits:

  • Maintain a healthy weight through balanced diet and regular physical activity
  • Limit intake of processed foods, sugary beverages, and high-fat foods
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption
  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly, especially if at risk for diabetes
  • Get regular check-ups and screenings for diabetes and related complications
