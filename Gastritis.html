Gastritis Information

About Gastritis

Gastritis is the inflammation of the lining of the stomach. It can occur suddenly (acute gastritis) or gradually over time (chronic gastritis). Gastritis can be caused by various factors, including bacterial infection, excessive alcohol consumption, chronic vomiting, stress, or the use of certain medications.

Causes of Gastritis

Gastritis can be caused by:

  • H. pylori bacteria infection
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Prolonged use of nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Chronic bile reflux
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Stress
  • Smoking
  • Severe injury or illness

Symptoms of Gastritis

  • Abdominal pain or discomfort
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Bloating
  • Loss of appetite
  • Indigestion
  • Belching
  • Heartburn
  • Blood in vomit or stool (severe cases)

Home Remedies for Gastritis

1. Avoid triggers such as spicy, acidic, or fatty foods.

2. Eat smaller, more frequent meals.

3. Avoid alcohol and caffeine.

4. Quit smoking.

5. Manage stress through relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.

6. Use over-the-counter antacids or acid reducers for symptom relief.

7. Drink chamomile tea or ginger tea to soothe the stomach.

Medications for Gastritis

Medications for gastritis may include:

  • Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce stomach acid production
  • H2-receptor antagonists to reduce acid production
  • Antibiotics to treat H. pylori infection
  • Antacids for symptom relief
  • Prokinetics to help the stomach empty more quickly

Diet Recommendations for Gastritis

During gastritis flare-ups, it's important to follow a bland diet and avoid irritating foods:

  • Plain foods like rice, boiled potatoes, and toast
  • Lean proteins such as grilled chicken or fish
  • Non-citrus fruits like bananas and apples
  • Vegetables that are well-cooked and non-spicy
  • Drink plenty of water and herbal teas

Avoid spicy, acidic, fatty, and fried foods that can exacerbate symptoms.
